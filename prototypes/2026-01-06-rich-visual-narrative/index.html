A guide to building software without an engineering background using the Jetty Method.

The problem with AI coding

If you've tried to code with natural language through AI assistants, you've probably run into some common challenges:

  • New features used to be a chat message away. Now simple improvements break old code.
  • You used to know exactly how your backend logic worked. Now you can't be 100% sure.
  • New functionality used to take minutes. Now you need an hour and 10x the tokens to make a button delete something.

In my initial attempts to code a software product by myself as a non-engineer, I ran into these problems again and again.

My background

10 years in product management taught me how to build features the right way. Just not how to build them myself.

My background wasn't completely useless. I had spent a lot of time around engineering as a 10 year veteran of product management at venture-funded startups. Part of what I did was help engineering teams build features the right way. In my world, the right way was a combination of principles and practices that outlined a combination of workflow frameworks.

When I first started to code with natural language at the end of 2024, I quickly realized that a new framework was needed to deal with unique challenges that come with AI assistants and coding as someone with, previously, no business coding.

✦

Altitude

The first thing I built with my new AI superpowers was called Nearish. It tried to reimagine what an online profile could look like for small businesses hoping to connect with would-be local patrons in a more personal way. I had a lot of different profile ideas so I decided to build several versions, test each of them, and go with what worked.

AI kept solving problems I didn't have yet

Things started simply enough. But throughout prototyping, my AI assistant started to worry about challenges that I didn't care about yet. Who could access these pages? What could users do if they were offline? What should sections with no content look like?

The birth of Speed Mode

This is just a prototype.

The number of times I'd say that became so frustrating that I unwittingly defined a set of temporary guidelines for my AI assistant that later became Speed Mode—a cornerstone of the Jetty Method that you'll soon learn.

Speed Mode: Build the happy path first. Worry about edge cases later.

Project after project, I discovered more about what AI was inherently bad at in the context of traditional development methodologies. Most importantly, I learned the engineering pitfalls that I was particularly prone to as a non-engineer.

The Jetty Method

The Jetty Method is the culmination of that learning, compiled for a new wave of non-technical Founders. It has helped me go from clueless vibe coder to shipping software projects for clients that were scalable and maintainable.
