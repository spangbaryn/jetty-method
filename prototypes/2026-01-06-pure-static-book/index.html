The old way of building software

For decades, building software meant learning to speak the language of computers. You had to understand syntax, memorize commands, and think in terms of loops, variables, and functions.

This created a barrier. If you had an idea for something useful, you either needed to spend years learning to code, or you needed to find (and pay) someone who already knew how.

What changed with AI

Large language models like Claude flipped the script. Instead of you learning to speak computer, the computer learned to understand you.

You can now describe what you want in plain English, and Claude will write the code. But here's what most people miss: this isn't magic, and it's not automatic. It's a skill.

A new skill: Communicating intent

The skill isn't coding anymore. The skill is clearly communicating your intent. What do you want to build? What should it do? What should happen when things go wrong?

This method teaches you how to have that conversation effectively.

Who this method is for

You don't need a computer science degree. You don't need to know what a variable is. You need curiosity, patience, and a willingness to iterate.

If you can explain what you want to a smart colleague, you can build software with Claude.
